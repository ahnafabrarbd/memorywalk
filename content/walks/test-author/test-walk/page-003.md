The light changes at the bend. The canal turns south and suddenly you are walking into the sun, low enough now to flatten everything into silhouette. The warehouses become shapes. The cranes on the far bank become lines. Your own shadow stretches behind you, thirty feet long and climbing the fence.

This is the moment when the walk starts to feel like something you are remembering rather than something you are doing.
