Past the warehouse the path narrows to single file. Scrub grass pushes through cracked tarmac. A shopping cart on its side, half-consumed by brambles, wheels still turning slightly when the wind catches them.

The sound here is different. The road noise from the bypass drops away and you can hear the canal water moving. Not flowing — moving. A thick, slow displacement of one dark surface against another.
